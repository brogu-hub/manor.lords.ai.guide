# Beginner's Guide — First Steps to a Thriving Settlement

## Getting Started

### Check the Map (Day 1, Paused)
- Survey region resources: Berries, Wild Animals, Salt, Iron, Fish, Clay, Stone
- Resources with **crowns** can be upgraded with Development Perks (Deep Mines = infinite)
- Check **Underground Water** overlay — Wells must be placed over water sources
- Check **Fertility** overlays: Emmer, Flax, Barley, Rye (dark green = best)
  - High fertility → farming (wheat → flour → bread)
  - Low fertility → Sheep Farms instead
- Flax → Linen (clothing), Barley → Malt → Ale (approval boost)

## Build Order

### Building #1: Logging Camp
- Raw Timber is the fundamental building block — build this first
- Place near town site, near significant wood, connected to roads
- Click on camp → **Assign a Logger** → Advanced Tab → **Limited Work Area** (focus one direction)
- Rotate logging sections yearly; build a Forester's Hut later to replant cleared areas
- Build roads around the Logging Camp

### Building #2: Hitching Post (relocate) + Buy 2nd Ox
- Relocate starting Hitching Post adjacent to Logging Camp
- Build a second Hitching Post and order a second ox (arrives in ~1 month)
- Oxen are Medieval work trucks — aim for 6-8 for a medium town, 2 is fine early

### Building #3: Well
- Place over Underground Water source
- Plan your town center around it: Well, Granary, Storehouse, Marketplace, Church, Tavern
- Burgages (homes) and production facilities go around the center

### Buildings #4-6: Granary + Storehouse + Marketplace (any order)
- Marketplace is a space, not a building — plan for 20-30 stalls
- Place Granary and Storehouse immediately nearby
- Assign 1 worker to Granary, 1 to Storehouse (temporary — remove after they pick up Settler Camp stores)
- Worker allocation: 3 of 5 assigned (1 Logger + 2 Warehouse), 2 free for building

### Building #7: 5 Long Burgages
- Build long for Vegetable Garden extensions (yard size affects yield)
- Costs 10 Timber total (2 per Burgage)
- Villagers move in automatically → no more homelessness penalty
- Families (wife + son) assist with work
- Build 2 Vegetable Plots at 15 Regional Wealth each with remaining wealth
- **You receive an UPGRADE point here — save it**

### Building #8: Woodcutter's Lodge
- Place near trees, anywhere near town
- Woodcutter works in a circle, takes trees slowly — relocate when exhausted
- Assign a Woodcutter → keep firewood between 10-40 for Year 1
- Unassign at 40 firewood — workers are precious early game
- Later: Charcoal Kilns (upgrade) dramatically increase firewood supply

### Building #9: Food Gatherer
- **Fish Ponds** (BEST) — single pond feeds settlement for years. With Pond Management perk + crown: ~1500 fish, accessible even in winter
- **Berries** (decent) — unavailable Nov-Mar. Also provides Dye and Herbs
- **Wild Animals** (decent) — depletes quickly. 20 animals, hunt 10 max before auto-stop. Also provides Hides → Leather (clothing)
- Assign a worker, gather ~50 food or as much as possible

### Building #10: Saw Pit
- Build next to Logging Camp, assign a worker
- Set Reserve of at least 5 Planks (preferably 10-12)
- Needed for Church construction (20 Planks)

### Building #11: Small Wooden Church
- Requires: 5 Timber, 10 Stone (from starting stockpile), 20 Planks
- Approval jumps over 50% → 1 new settler per month (on the 2nd)
- New settlers auto-move into vacant Burgages — **must have vacancy**

### Building #12: New Burgages (at least 1 vacant)
- Build near town center, can be half-length of veggie plot ones
- Need enough space behind house for workshops/food production (dashed line)
- Vacancy required to receive new settlers

### Building #13: Trading Post
- Place near Storehouse/Granary and a King's Road
- **Surround with roads on all 4 sides** (finicky requirement)
- Assign a worker, set up trade routes (Planks + gathered resources)
- Set minimum amounts so you keep what you need
- **Buy a Horse at $20** — greatly speeds up trade
- At $15+ wealth: build Animal Plots on Burgages → buy Goats (not pigs)
  - Goats: 1 Meat + 1 Hide every 5 months
  - Build a Tannery to process Hides → Leather → Marketplace

### Building #14: Stonecutter
- Need Stone resource in region (otherwise mine Salt/Iron/Clay and trade for Stone)
- Target: 25 Stone for Manor House

### Building #15: Manor House
- Requires: 25 Stone, 5 Timber, 20 Planks
- Functions: Tax rate (Personal Wealth), Policies, Castle building
- **Gives FREE Retinue of 5 soldiers** (expandable to 12, or 24 with Garrison Tower)
- Essential for early defense — build on a hill if possible

## Combat: Kill the Bandits

1. Rally military: 5 Retinue + 20 Spear Militia (default start) = 25 men
2. Find Bandit Camp → 18 bandits come out to fight
3. **Tactics:** Set Retinue on a hill → let bandits come → flank with Spear Militia from behind
4. Send Militia home and deactivate. Send Retinue to plunder camp, then home
5. **ALWAYS take plunder for Personal Use** (not Regional Wealth)

### Why Personal Wealth matters:
- Buy more Retinue soldiers: $50 each
- Hire Mercenaries
- Send Settlers to Claimed Regions: $250

Keep attacking new Bandit Camps whenever they appear. Winter mobilization is better (less farm work).

## Clothing Sources (Priority Order)

1. **Wild Animals** → Hunters provide Hides → Tannery → Leather (easiest)
2. **Burgage Goat Plots** — 2 Planks + 15 Regional Wealth each (simplest)
3. **Sheep Farm** — requires purchasing sheep ($20-30 each) + Weaver Shop
4. **Flax Farm** → Weaver Shop → Linen (very labor intensive)
5. **Import via Trader** (expensive, unreliable)

## Development Perks Guide

### Foraging Perks
| Perk | Rating | Notes |
|------|--------|-------|
| **Pond Management** | S-TIER | Fish pond with crown = ~1500 fish, even in winter. No-brainer. |
| Beekeeping | Good | 2 Apiaries, up to 700 honey/year. Good for food + trade. |
| Forest Management | OK | Only affects berry max capacity, not growth rate. Easy to over-harvest. |
| Trapping | Skip | ~10 extra meat/year. Not worth it. |

### Farming Perks
| Perk | Rating | Notes |
|------|--------|-------|
| **Plough** | Great | Oxen-assisted farming is massively faster. Need this if farming-heavy. |
| **Orchardry** | Great | Long game (3-4 years to grow). 3 apples per 2 trees/year. Good for food + trade. |
| Sheep | Good | Herds grow free. Butcher shop for overcrowding. Drowning in wool. |
| Bakeries | Good | 4 bread from 1 flour at Bakery Burgage upgrade. Pairs with wheat/rye. |
| Rye | Niche | Grain in low fertility regions. Requires Orchardry first. |

### Mining/Production Perks
| Perk | Rating | Notes |
|------|--------|-------|
| **Deep Mines** | Great | Requires Charcoal Kilns. Crown resources become infinite. Kilns = tons of firewood. |
| Armor Making | Skip | Rarely useful. |

### Trading Perks
- Not recommended.

## Key Tips

- **Fish Pond is king** — restart until you get one if needed
- **Ox early, ox often** — 6-8 oxen for a medium town
- **Bandit plunder → always Personal Wealth** — you need it for retinue ($50 each) and settlers ($250)
- Approval over 50% = 1 new settler/month. Over 75% = faster growth
- Firewood target: 10-40 for Year 1, scale up after
- **Winter mobilization** — fight bandits in winter when workers have less farm work
- Burgages need Church coverage + Marketplace stalls + Clothing to upgrade to Level 2
